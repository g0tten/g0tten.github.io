About the project

Gotten generates domain-specific metamorphic testing environments from model-driven artefacts.

The framework combines meta-models, domain-specific languages, and domain processors to support the systematic definition, execution, and reporting of metamorphic tests, together with the automated generation of follow-up test cases.

Core capabilities

  • Define input, output, and processor features with mrDSL.
  • Specify metamorphic relations over domain concepts and system executions.
  • Generate follow-up test cases through search-based strategies expressed in fowDSL.
  • Reuse the same framework across different domains while retaining domain-specific models.

Available examples

  • Cloud simulators, with relations involving CPU, network, memory, energy consumption, and execution time.
  • Video streaming APIs, with relations involving searches, updates, and result sets.
  • Autonomous vehicles, with relations involving nominal speed, obstacles, and reference waypoints.
  • Finite automata, with relations involving automaton models and words to be processed.
  • Public repositories and downloadable sample projects for these domains.

Authors and contributors

Gotten has been developed by Pablo Gómez-Abajo, Pablo C. Cañizares, Alberto Núñez, Esther Guerra, and Juan de Lara. Its technical foundations build on frameworks including Xtext, Henshin, and MOMoT.
